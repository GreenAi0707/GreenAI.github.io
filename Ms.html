Research Areas

    1. Green AI

    Rapid growth in energy demand and rising environmental concerns have highlighted the need for smarter and more sustainable energy systems. Traditional approaches often rely heavily on conventional resources and lack the intelligence to efficiently utilize renewables. In response, this work aims to develop intelligent energy management frameworks by integrating renewable energy systems with advanced Machine Learning and Reinforcement Learning techniques, aiming to maximize the use of renewable energy while minimizing dependence on conventional sources through optimal scheduling and control of Distributed Energy Resources (DERs), thereby contributing to cleaner, more efficient, and sustainable energy systems.

    2. Demand-Side Flexibility

    The increasing penetration of renewable energy resources on both the demand and supply sides has created new challenges for maintaining grid stability and reliability. As the share of renewables grows, balancing supply and demand from the generation side alone becomes increasingly difficult. To respond to this problem, this work aims to enhance power system flexibility by leveraging demand-side resources through the development of optimization and control strategies for DERs installed at the consumer level, enabling dynamic load adjustment, demand response, and efficient energy usage, while supporting grid stability and empowering consumers to actively participate in energy management.

    3. Building Energy System Design

    Rapid urbanization and increasing energy demand in residential and commercial buildings have highlighted the need for efficient, reliable, and cost-effective energy solutions. Traditional building energy systems often operate with limited coordination among resources, leading to inefficiencies and higher operational costs. In response, this research focuses on designing multi-energy systems by combining multiple DERs, including optimal sizing, configuration, and operation to reliably meet energy demand. The objective is to enhance energy efficiency and significantly reduce operational costs for consumers through intelligent system design and optimization.
